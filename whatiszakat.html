Understand your true Zakat

What is Zakat

Zakat is one of the foundations of our religion: a means for purifying our wealth and taking care of our society.

When you hoard your money, you belong to it, but when you spend it, then it belongs to you -Al Ahnaf ibn Qays

Is it wrong to be rich in Islam? Is poverty a virtue?

The Prophet (PBUH) said, "For you to leave your family wealthy is better than leaving them dependent and begging from people. Everything you spend on them is a Sadaqa, even the morslef of food you place in your wife's mouth"

Being rich is not a sin, but not caring for your family is. We should all strive to care for our families the best we can. Poverty was something the the Prophet (PBUH) would consistenyl seek refuge from. Poverty is an abject state, where you find yourself wanting of others. You may be dissatisfied and be driven to sinful action because of it. We should not seek to be poor, nor should we chase endlessly after the riches of this life. Instead, we should look at the allure of food. Eat too little and you will be malnourished, too much and you will be sluggish and apathetic. Life is a test, and we are stewards to this world. The wealth that we are given (or not) is a means for us to pass that test. Allah SWT says in the Quran, "You will never attain piety until you spend from what you love. And anything you spend, and God knows it."

Zakat Basic Concepts

1. Zakat is not progressive. It's a flat rate on your savings after expenses.

2. Zakat is a purification of your wealth. You are doing one of three things: conserving the world's resources, preventing the larger problem of need from affecting society as a whole.

3. You only pay on what you own and control. First, you have to own it yourself, and not be tangled up in an investment where the profit and loss isn't clear. The only money you should pay Zakat on is what you own, and is surplus of your needs. You shouldn't pay Zakat on your personal assets (i.e. your car, your house, or your sofa)

4. You only pay on liquid wealth you can expect to benefit from. For example, currency, livestock, and crop yields will increase if invested or traded.

5. You only pay on surplus wealth held for more than a year. An entire lunar year should pass on any wealth or money that you own. There are a few exceptions to this rule, such as payouts from businesses or investments made. When you calculate your zakat, make sure you've had a surplus (above the nisab) the duration of the lunar year, then pay on your total wealth of savings after the year accordingly.
