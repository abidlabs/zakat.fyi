1c. Understand the Hawl

The hawl is the the Zakat Year, like a fiscal year, but used to calculate your zakat. This is the lunar year, 11 days shorter than the regular year. Look back in your account history. You start calculating Zakat on the day your account was greater than the ni·π£ƒÅb. If that amount remained consistently above the ni·π£ƒÅb for one year, you must pay Zakat on your total liable wealth. To keep things simple, you can set your hawl date on the day you calculated or paid Zakat last year.

If you're worried your total liable wealth dropped below $318.23 sometime during the hawl, double check using a snapshot of the dates in question on the calculator below. Otherwise, continue through the calculator with your current financial snapshot, and we'll let you know if and how much you owe in Zakat!

1d. You vs. You + Your Spouse

The zakat is due by the person who owns the wealth, whether that be man or woman. If you choose, you can pay zakat on behalf of your spouse, in which case, make sure to the sum the total for both spouses for each of these questions.

2j. Post-tax 401(k) distributions üë¥

Joe Bradford's recommended Zakah calculation is to pay Zakah only on what has your full control, or what you have withdrawn. This is treating your 401k like a passive investment. If you reach retirement age and cash out, you will pay zakat only ONE year on the entire amount, and then EVERY year after because you will have unfettered access to the full amount. If you withdraw early, then you will pay zakat on the NET amount you withdraw after taxes and penalties.

If these distributions are already in your checking account or another account previously listed, be sure not to double count them.

2s. Aggregate Cash Value of Inventory üì¶

First, you pay zakat on all of the items in your inventory, including raw ingredients and final products that you have not yet sold. Of course, you must have owned the items for the hawl. In the case of a partnership, value your share of the inventory.

Wholesalers should use the wholesale price in their calculation, and retailers should use the retail price.

2v. Good Debt (Owed to You) üí±

Good Debt is debt that you are owed and expect to collect. If something prevents you from doing so, like the inability of a person to pay or their refusal to pay, it becomes Bad Debt. You are liable to pay zakat on a Bad Debt when you actually receive it.

$

Expenses and Liabilites

When calculating your zakat liable amount, you should remove the amount of money that you owe to others. In addition to debts, you can also deduct bills that are "immediately due." For example, if you pay your rent monthly, you can deduct one month of your rent. If you pay your insurance every 6 months, and your insurance payment is up immediately, you can deduct the 6 month insurance bill.
