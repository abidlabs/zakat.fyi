1b. Let's Calculate our Niṣāb

The niṣāb is the minimum (liable) amount that a Muslim must have to be obligated to pay Zakah. This amount was set by our Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) at 595 grams of Silver. Today, the price of Silver per gram is S. Therefore, the niṣāb is N.

1c. Let's Set our Hawl

The hawl is the the Zakat Year, like a fiscal year, but dealing with your Zakat. This is the lunar year, 11 days shorter than the regular year. Look back in your account history. You start calculating Zakat on the day your account was greater than the niṣāb. If that amount remained consistently above the niṣāb for one year, you must pay Zakat on your total liable wealth. To keep things simple, you can set your hawl date on the day you calculated or paid Zakah last year.

If you're worried your total liable wealth dropped below N sometime during the hawl, double check using a snapshot of the dates in question on the calculator below. Otherwise, continue through the calculator with your current financial snapshot, and we'll let you know if and how much you owe in Zakah!

2j. Post-tax 401(k) distributions 👴

Joe Bradford's recommended Zakah calculation is to pay Zakah only on what has your full control, or what you have withdrawn. This is treating your 401k like a passive investment. If you reach retirement age and cash out, you will pay zakat only ONE year on the entire amount, and then EVERY year after because you will have unfettered access to the full amount. If you withdraw early, then you will pay zakat on the NET amount you withdraw after taxes and penalties

If you have a piece of property or real estate that is highly illiquid and the price is not readily determinable because of how unique or rare it is, treat it the same as a passive investment. When you sell, pay zakat. Otherwise, move on to the next question.

2r. Rental Income 🏘️

If you rent a property, you do not need to pay Zakah on the value of the property itself. The property will count as the tool of your trade, rather than the result.

If you added your rental income to your general checking or savings accounts, do not double count the earnings here. However, if you have a separate account for rental income, add that value in here.

2v. Good Debt 💱

Good Debt is debt that you are owed and expect to collect. If something prevents you from doing so, like the inability of a person to pay or their refusal to pay, it becomes Bad Debt. You are liable to pay zakat on a Bad Debt when you actually receive it.

4a. Monthly Living Expenses 🍔

This can include: rent/mortgage payments, medical expenses (other than your insurance premium), utilities, groceries, transport, upkeep, and any other miscellaneous expenses. Because your bills and expenses are considered debts owed to others, you have to pay those first. You should only deduct those expenses which are immediately due.
