1c. Let's Set our Hawl

The hawl is the the Zakat Year, like a fiscal year, but used to calculate your zakat. This is the lunar year, 11 days shorter than the regular year. Look back in your account history. You start calculating Zakat on the day your account was greater than the ni·π£ƒÅb. If that amount remained consistently above the ni·π£ƒÅb for one year, you must pay Zakat on your total liable wealth. To keep things simple, you can set your hawl date on the day you calculated or paid Zakat last year.

If you're worried your total liable wealth dropped below N sometime during the hawl, double check using a snapshot of the dates in question on the calculator below. Otherwise, continue through the calculator with your current financial snapshot, and we'll let you know if and how much you owe in Zakat!

2j. Post-tax 401(k) distributions üë¥

Joe Bradford's recommended Zakat calculation is to calculate Zakat only on what has your full control, or what you have withdrawn. This is treating your 401k like passive investment. If you reach retirement age and cash out, you will pay zakat only one year on the entire amount, and then every year after because you will have unfettered access to the full amount. If you withdraw early, then you will pay zakat on the net amount you withdraw after taxes and penalties
